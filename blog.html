Why do cats sleep a lot?

Normally cats sleep an average of 15 hours per days. My Ginger probably sleeps 20 hours and spends last 4 hours to meow. Cats sleep to conserve energy. Sure, other fur-covered mammals coast by on 8 hours of sleep per cycle, but cats may sleep to help regulate body temperature, as well as for pleasure. Wild cats need to sleep in order to conserve their energy to hunt, chase and kill their next meal. Although our house cats may not need to hunt, the instinct to sleep and prepare for the hunt carries on.
